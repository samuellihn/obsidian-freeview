Gaussian Surfaces

The two most common Gaussian surfaces used in Gauss's Law are spheres and cylinders
Spheres are used for point or spherical charges, as will be uniform if the point charge is centered

Volume
Surface Area

Cylinders are used for charged rods where on the outside of the sphere will be uniform
The flux going through the top and bottom will be zero, because the surface normal and electric field vector are perpendicular
